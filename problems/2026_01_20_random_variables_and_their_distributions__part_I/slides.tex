\begin{frame}{Terminology Note}

Given a random variable $X$, I use the conventional terminology
\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{Cumulative distribution function} to refer to $F_X \defeq P(X \leq x)$
	\item \textbf{Probability mass function} to refer to $f_X \defeq P(X=x)$ when $X$ is discrete.
	\item \textbf{Probability density function} to refer to $f_X$ such that $P(X \leq x) = \int_{-\infty}^x f_X(t) \wrt{t}$ when $X$ is continuous. 
\end{enumerate}

And I use the term \textbf{probability distribution} to refer broadly to any of these, since each is sufficient to fully characterize the behavior of the random variable.
	
\end{frame}

\item  (2.1.6) \textbf{Uniform Distribution.}  A random variable that is equally likely to take any value in a finite set $S$ is said to to have the \textit{uniform distribution} on $S$.  If $U$ is such a random variable and $\emptyset \neq R \subseteq S$, show that the distribution of $U$ conditional on $\set{U \in R}$ is uniform in $R$. 
\item (2.2.3) Let $\set{X_r : r \geq 1}$ be observations which are independent and identically distributed with unknown distribution function $F$.  Describe and justify a method for estimating $F(x)$. 
\item (2.3.4) Show that, if $f$ and $g$ are density functions, and $0 \leq \lambda \leq 1$, then $\lambda f + (1-\lambda) g$ is a density.  Is the product $fg$ a density function?
\end{enumerate}
